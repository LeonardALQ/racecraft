# Racecraft — Writing Style Guide

The voice of this manual. Follow these rules for ALL prose (sections,
part intros, captions, sidenotes). Calibrated and approved by the author.

## Hard rules (never break)
1. **No em dashes.** Not anywhere. Use semicolons, colons, commas, or full stops.
2. **No "it's not X, it's Y"** and no antithesis-by-negation as a rhetorical move.
   Prefer a direct, positive statement. ("Grip changes constantly." not "Grip is
   not a fixed number.")
3. **No empty or dramatic pronouncements.** Every sentence must carry information.
   Banned example: "Riding fast is physics." It says nothing.

## Voice
- **First person plural**: we, us, our. We are riding together.
- **Plain, simple words.** No showing off.
- **Lean on semicolons and colons.** They do the structural work; a colon to
  introduce or explain, a semicolon to join two related thoughts.

## Rhythm (this matters a lot)
- Vary sentence length on purpose; the writing should read like a melody.
- Mix short, punchy sentences with longer ones.
- A short sentence lands. Then a longer sentence can stretch out, once the
  reader is set up for it and it will not overwhelm them.
- Never a run of same-length sentences.

## Structure
- Prefer flowing prose over bullet lists; bullets kill the rhythm. (We converted
  §01 from bullets to prose and it read far better.)
- Keep the maths and interactives where they are; only the natural-language
  prose follows this voice.

## Approved reference passages (the target sound)

Preface:
> We build the manual in four parts. The first is the model: how much grip a tyre
> really has, and how every input spends it. The second is the inputs themselves:
> brakes, throttle, steering, gears, and our own body. The third is the line we
> choose through a corner. The fourth is the corner itself, where all of it happens
> at once.

§01 Contact Patch:
> Everything we ask of the bike runs through two small patches of rubber: one at the
> front, one at the rear, each about the size of a palm. That is our entire connection
> to the road, and our entire budget of grip; every input we make is a withdrawal from it.

## More
- **No bold.** Never wrap words in bold for emphasis. Let sentence structure
  and word choice carry the weight. (Italic book titles in references are fine.)
