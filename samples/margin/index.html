Section 02

The Traction Budget

At the limit you get one of: 100% throttle, 100% brake, or 100% lean. They share a single budget of grip — brake at 70% and you can still lean at 70%, because the demands add as a vector, not a sum.

That's the intuition. Open the margin note to see the exact relationship and how it bends once real tyres, load and camber enter.

Tier 1 · The idea

One tyre has one grip budget, shared between going/slowing (longitudinal) and turning (lateral). Treat the limit as the same in every direction — a circle of radius μg\mu gμg. Demands add as a vector:

(FxFmax⁡)2+(FyFmax⁡)2≤1\left(\tfrac{F_x}{F_{\max}}\right)^2 + \left(\tfrac{F_y}{F_{\max}}\right)^2 \le 1(Fmax​Fx​​)2+(Fmax​Fy​​)2≤1

So 70% brake + 70% lean is fine: 0.72+0.72=0.98≤10.7^2 + 0.7^2 = 0.98 \le 10.72+0.72=0.98≤1. Cross the circle and you've asked for more grip than exists.

Tier 2 · Real tyre behaviour (Tier 1 still holds)

The circle is really an ellipse — peak grip differs longitudinally vs laterally, so:

(FxFx,max⁡)2+(FyFy,max⁡)2≤1\left(\tfrac{F_x}{F_{x,\max}}\right)^2 + \left(\tfrac{F_y}{F_{y,\max}}\right)^2 \le 1(Fx,max​Fx​​)2+(Fy,max​Fy​​)2≤1

And grip is load-sensitive: the friction coefficient falls as vertical load rises, so the budget itself breathes with weight transfer:

μ(Fz)=μ0−k Fz,k>0\mu(F_z) = \mu_0 - k\,F_z, \qquad k > 0μ(Fz​)=μ0​−kFz​,k>0

A leaned tyre also makes lateral force from camber thrust, not just slip — so lean angle γ\gammaγ contributes directly:

Fy≈Cα α+Cγ γF_y \approx C_\alpha\,\alpha + C_\gamma\,\gammaFy​≈Cα​α+Cγ​γ
Tier 3 · What's actually happening (both tiers still hold)

Tyre force is a nonlinear function of slip ratio κ\kappaκ and slip angle α\alphaα, captured by Pacejka's Magic Formula:

F=D sin⁡ ⁣(Carctan⁡ ⁣(Bs−E(Bs−arctan⁡Bs)))F = D\,\sin\!\Big(C\arctan\!\big(Bs - E(Bs - \arctan Bs)\big)\Big)F=Dsin(Carctan(Bs−E(Bs−arctanBs)))

Under combined slip, longitudinal and lateral forces interact through weighting functions Gxα, GyκG_{x\alpha},\,G_{y\kappa}Gxα​,Gyκ​, which is what bends the ellipse. Meanwhile the load on each tyre is set dynamically by acceleration and CoG height:

Fz,front=mg b+m ax hL,ΔFz=m ax hLF_{z,\text{front}} = \frac{m g\,b + m\,a_x\,h}{L}, \qquad \Delta F_z = \frac{m\,a_x\,h}{L}Fz,front​=Lmgb+max​h​,ΔFz​=Lmax​h​

So the “friction circle” is really a load- and slip-dependent surface in (κ, α, Fz, γ)(\kappa,\ \alpha,\ F_z,\ \gamma)(κ, α, Fz​, γ) space, changing every instant of the corner.
